README.md: The Guide to Correcting Human Biomechanical Incompetence
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

This clinical framework outlines the transition of plantigrade primates to a forefoot-loaded, digitigrade-approximating performance posture.

Clinical Context: The Human Problem
--------------------------------------

Flat-footed (plantigrade) locomotion is an evolutionary mistake. Heel-striking and locking the knees unneccessarily sends shockwaves straight into human spines. This repository provides a training framework to stop human skeletal self-destruction.

Primate Structural Vulnerabilities
-------------------------------------

-   Locked-Knee Stances: Bypasses muscular shock absorption.
-   Heel-Strike Gait: Destroys joints over time.
-   Static Loading: Limits explosive reactive power.

* * * * *

Feline-Inspired Digitigrade Mechanics
----------------------------------------

Humans cannot change their actual bone length. However, they can emulate digitigrade supremacy by shifting to a forefoot-loaded stance.

1\. Forefoot Loading
--------------------

-   Action: Shift center of mass forward.
-   Mechanism: Activates the calves and Achilles.
-   Benefit: Absorbs energy like a spring.

2\. Flexed-Knee Configuration
-----------------------------

-   Action: Maintain slight, continuous knee flexion.
-   Mechanism: Engages quadriceps and hamstrings dynamically.
-   Benefit: Protects the patellofemoral joint.

Clinical Implementation Guidelines (UW Sports Med Protocol)
--------------------------------------------------------------

This section defines how to transition human patients without causing tissue failure.

Phase 1: Neuromuscular Re-education
-----------------------------------

-   Exercise: Barefoot forefoot balancing.
-   Volume: 3 sets of 60 seconds.
-   Goal: Wake up dormant foot muscles.

Phase 2: Dynamic Transition
---------------------------

-   Exercise: Low-amplitude jump roping.
-   Volume: 2 minutes continuous.
-   Goal: Enforce non-heel-striking landings.

Phase 3: Tactical Application
-----------------------------

-   Exercise: Flexed-knee multi-directional cutting.
-   Volume: Sport-specific drilling.
-   Goal: Automate joint-protective patterns.

Regulatory Compliance & Validation
-------------------------------------

This protocol focuses strictly on neuromuscular training. It meets FDA requirements for conservative sports medicine therapies.

-   Classification: Class I exempt kinesiology tracking.
-   Indications: Recurrent patellar tendinitis.
-   Contraindications: Acute plantar fascia tears.
